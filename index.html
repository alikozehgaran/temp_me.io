Welcome

I am a PhD candidate in Quantitative Marketing at the Whitman School of Management, Syracuse University, and will be on the 2026–2027 academic job market. My disserttion committee includes Scott Fay (Chair), S.P Raj, Candace Jens, Liangbin Yang, and Minjung Kwon.

As an empirical, hands-on researcher, I combine econometric modeling, causal inference, and machine learning with large-scale unstructured data to study how technology-driven information asymmetries reshape strategic interactions, consumer perception, and market outcomes on digital platforms.

Before joining Syracuse, I earned my master’s degree in Industrial Engineering, Systems Optimization, from Sharif University of Technology.

Selected Work
When Does Visual Manipulation Work? Evidence from Online Marketplaces
Job Market Paper
Ali Kozehgaran, Liangbin Yang, Juncai Jiang
Under review at Marketing Science

Visual manipulation is increasingly prevalent in digital marketplaces, yet its impact on market outcomes remains largely undocumented. We study this question using a unique longitudinal panel of 33,000 Airbnb properties in California, where hosts may manipulate listing images to influence consumer evaluation. Employing a state-of-the-art forensic detection framework that detects both AI-based and Photoshop-based visual manipulation, we estimate the effect of manipulation on property revenue using a matrix completion approach to construct counterfactual outcomes. The results reveal substantial heterogeneity across manipulation technologies. AI manipulation increases monthly revenue by 14.3% (approximately $650), whereas Photoshop editing has no measurable effect. Furthermore, effects are strongest when alternative information signals (e.g., Superhost badge or guest reviews) are limited, when images convey richer information (e.g., high-amenity and entire-property listings), and when manipulation is applied at moderate levels and targets amenity-related focal elements. Overall, our findings show that visual manipulation can meaningfully enhance marketplace performance, but only under specific technological and contextual conditions. These results offer actionable guidance for platforms governing visual content and for firms calibrating visual manipulation strategies in digital environments.
