Teaching Philosophy — The EPIC Framework

My teaching rests on four commitments I call EPIC: Engagement, Practical Learning, Inclusivity, and Critical Thinking. These principles have guided my teaching across Principles of Marketing, Essentials of Marketing, and Brand Management.

E
Engagement
A bonus point system rewards contributions — questions, peer responses, and observations. Mid-lecture QR-code prompts via Microsoft Forms spark real-time reflection and discussion. In Spring 2025, in-class participation rose 73% year-over-year.
P
Practical Learning
Every major assignment bridges theory and practice. The Mini Presentation assignment asks students to analyze curated business videos, research supporting evidence, and deliver concise analytical talks — over 300 minutes of peer presentations per semester.
I
Inclusivity
Anonymous QR-code discussions create a judgment-free space. I meet individually with each student at least once per semester for feedback and mentorship. Lectures are redesigned with cross-disciplinary examples for non-business majors.
C
Critical Thinking
Assessments move beyond memorization. Students compare strategies, identify weaknesses, and formulate predictions. In Brand Management, the Markstrat simulation demands strategic reasoning under shifting competitive dynamics.
